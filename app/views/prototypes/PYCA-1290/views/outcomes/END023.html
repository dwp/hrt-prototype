Claimant status

EEA worker, Redundant or Temporarily sick EEA citizen

Go to the ‘check if HRT interview is needed’ to-do

  1. For the question “Does the ‘prove you can apply’ service say ‘this person has proved they can apply for Universal Credit’?”, select ‘No’.
  2. Select the category ‘Fast-track this person’.
  3. Select ‘Done’.

Complete the ‘refer to decision maker (HRT)’ to-do

  1. Download the ALP.
  2. Complete the appropriate section in the ALP using the necessary evidence.
  3. Upload the ALP.
  4. Enter the claimant’s nationality.
  5. Select ‘Done’.

Evidence to record in the ALP

One of the following:

  • passport
  • national ID card

Include number, nationality, issue date and expiry date.

All of the following:

  • redundancy letter from employer

Include employer name, date of letter, date employment ended or will end, and amount of redundancy payment.

Plus one from the following list:

  • payslips covering their final 3 months of employment – name of employer and earnings (including pay frequency, date and amount)
  • bank statements showing earnings from their final 3 months of employment (including pay dates and amounts)
  • payslips or bank statements covering their final month of employment, and contract or letter from their employer confirming type and duration of contract, start date, hours of work and earnings
